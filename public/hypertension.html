Hypertension

Hypertension means an increase in blood pressure beyond healthy levels. There are a multitude of causes of hypertension, but many stem from the narrowing of blood vessels. The normal blood pressure is around 110/70. In severe hypertension, blood pressure can rise up to 140/90. Using the formula MAP = SVR * CO, we found that SVR in hypertension increased by a factor of 1.68 mmHg*s/mL, so we modeled the new simulation based on an SVR of 1.68. Also, the initial arterial pressure is raised to 90 (diastolic blood pressure in hypertension) in this simulation.
