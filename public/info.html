CardioVision

CardioVision Guide

What is CardioVision?

CardioVision is a dynamic web application that uses a 6th degree vector polynomial function to generate a simulation of a single cardiac cycle. The difference between CardioVision and other cardiovascular graphing technologies is its ability to take in custom cardiac parameters specific to an individual patient and use differential equations to produce graphs of their cardiac cycle through instantaneous changes.

What are the uses of CardioVision?

CardioVision is the first online software to generate custom, user-generated cardiac cycle graphs. CardioVision is great for use in the medical classroom, as it allows medical students to visulize the way changes in cardiac parameters in turn cause changes in the cardiac cycle graphs in aspects such as shape, pressures, volumes, etc. Beyond medical education, CardioVision can be used in the medical setting for health practitioners to communicate with patients regarding the impacts of their cardiovascular treatment with a visualization tool, facilitating physician-patient communication.

What do each of the parameters mean?

  • Heart Rate (bpm): The number of times the heart beats in 1 minute.
  • Left Ventricular Pressure (mmHg): Also known as the LVP, this is a measure of the blood pressure against the left ventricle. The initial LVP is the LVP at the beginning of each cardiac cycle.
  • Left Atrial Pressure (mmHg): Also known as the LAP, it is a measure of the blood pressure against the left atrium. The initial LAP is the LAP at the beginning of each cardiac cycle.
  • Aortic Pressure (mmHg): Also known as the AOP, this measures the blood pressure in the aorta, the artery that receives blood from the left ventricle.
  • Arterial Pressure (mmHg): Also known as the AP, this measures the blood pressure in peripheral arteries.
  • Systemic Vascular Resistance (mmHg*s/mL): Also known as the SVR, this refers to the resistance to blood flow offered by all of the systemic vasculature, excluding the pulmonary vasculature. It can be calculated as the mean arterial pressure divided by the cardiac output.
  • Mitral Valve Resistance (mmHg*s/mL): The mitral valve is the valve between the left atrium and left ventricle. Its diameter greatly affects the blood resistance and in turn, the ability of the left atrium to pump blood into the left ventricle.
  • Aortic Valve Resistance (mmHg*s/mL): The aortic valve is the valve between the left ventricle and the aorta respsonble for allowing the left ventricle to pump blood to the rest of the body. Its diameter greatly affects the resistance of blood flowing through it, and in turn, the ability of the left ventricle to pump blood throughout the body.
