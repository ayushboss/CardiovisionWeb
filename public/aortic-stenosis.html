Aortic Stenosis

Aortic stenosis is the narrowing of the aortic valve, the valve facilitating blood flow from the left ventricle to the aorta, pumping blood to the rest of the body. In aortic stenosis, the aortic valve area is decreased by a factor of 4, so the resistance should be increased by a power of 16. We modeled the aortic resistance as 0.0160 instead of 0.001 mmHg*s/mL.

Observations:
  • Left ventricular pressure is much higher due to the accumulated blood that cannot be expelled to the aorta; as a result, aortic and arterial pressure are both much lower
  • Lower blood flow rate because less blood flow
  • Higher left ventricular volume due to hypertrophy
  • Flow rate and general cardiac pressures begin to stay static due to the compensation of left ventricular hypertrophy
