Mitral Stenosis

Mitral stenosis is the narrowing of the mitral valve, meaning a decreased mitral valve diameter. The exact cause of mitral stenosis is unknown. Mitral stenosis entails increased mitral resistance. In critical mitral stenosis, the valve area is approximately halved, making the resistance approximately quadrupled. So, to model the mitral stenosis curve, we use a resistance of 0.02 instead of 0.005 mmHg*s/mL.

Common symptoms of mitral stenosis include: fatigue, shortness of breath, swollen ankles or feet, heart paliptations, etc. To learn more about mitral stenosis, visit here.

Observations:
  • Increased left atrial pressure, prolonged will lead to left atrial hypertrophy
  • Over several heartbeats, there is a gradual increase in atrial pressure (due to blood accumulation) and a gradual decrease in the rest of the values, as it is less blood is pumped into the ventricles and later into the arteries and aorta
  • Rapid decline in blood flow rate, then a gradual increase as the heart adapts to a narrower mitral valve
