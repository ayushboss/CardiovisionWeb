Hypotension

Hypotension means a decrease in blood pressure below healthy levels. There are a multitude of causes of hypertension, but many stem from the widening of blood vessels. The blood pressure of a normal patient is usually around 110/70. In severe hypotension, the blood pressure can decrease to about 80/50. Again, using the equation MAP = SVR * CO, we found that the SVR is now modeled as 0.643 mmHg*s/mL. Also, the initial arterial pressure modeled is 50 mmHg.
